    How to make Biriyani Receipe

  1. Gather all ingredients :

    1. Chicken
    2. Basmati rice
    3. onions
    4. tomatoes
    5. spices oil
    6. yogurt
    7. ghee
    8. salt
    9. saffron
    10. milk
    11. ginger
    12. garlic
    13. green chilies
    14. mint
    15. lemon

  2. Now start to cooking :
    1. Wash the basmati rice thoroughly until water runs clear.
    2. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes while you prepare other ingredients.
    3. Wash and pat dry the chicken pieces.
    4. Make a paste of ginger and garlic (about 2 tablespoons).
    5. Finely slice 2 large onions into thin rings.
    6. Heat oil in a skillet and fry half the onions until golden brown; drain and set aside (for garnish).
    7. In a bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, coriander powder, salt, and lemon juice.
    8. Add the chicken pieces to the yogurt marinade and coat well.
    9. Marinate the chicken for at least 1 hour (overnight gives best results).
    10. Chop tomatoes into small pieces and keep aside.
    11. Chop cilantro (coriander) and mint leaves and set aside.
    12. Soak a pinch of saffron in 2 tablespoons of warm milk and set aside to infuse color.
    13. Bring a large pot of water to a boil; add salt and whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon stick, cloves, green cardamom, black peppercorns).
    14. Drain the soaked rice and add to boiling water; cook until rice is 70% done (grains should still have a slight bite).
    15. Drain rice and spread on a tray to stop cooking.
    16. In a heavy-bottomed pot (dutch oven), heat ghee and a tablespoon of oil.
    17. Add whole spices (cinnamon, cardamom, cloves) and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
    18. Add chopped onions (the remaining half) and sauté until translucent.
    19. Add marinated chicken to the pot and sear on medium-high heat for 4–6 minutes to lock flavors.
    20. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they break down and oil separates.
    21. Check seasoning and add salt if needed.
    22. Add chopped cilantro and mint to the chicken layer and mix lightly.
    23. Lower heat to medium and add half of the partially cooked rice over the chicken to create the first layer.
    24. Sprinkle some fried onions, chopped mint, and cilantro on top of the rice layer.
    25. Drizzle half of the saffron milk and a tablespoon of ghee over the rice layer.
    26. Carefully add the remaining rice to form the second layer.
    27. Top the rice with the remaining fried onions, mint, cilantro, and saffron milk.
    28. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid. If the lid isn't tight, seal edges with dough to trap steam (optional).
    29. Place a griddle/tawa on medium heat and put the pot on top (this prevents direct heat and avoids burning).
    30. Cook on low heat (dum) for 25–30 minutes to let flavors meld and rice finish cooking.
    31. After 20 minutes, check aroma; add a few drops of rose water or kewra water for extra fragrance (optional).
    32. Once done, turn off the heat and let the pot rest for 10 minutes before opening.
    33. Gently fluff the biryani with a fork to mix layers without breaking rice.
    34. Serve biryani on a large platter and garnish with toasted nuts (optional).
    35. Accompany with raita (yogurt-cucumber), lemon wedges, and salad.
    36. Leftovers: store in refrigerator in an airtight container for up to 2 days.
    37. Reheating tip: sprinkle a little water and heat covered on low until warm, or microwave with a damp paper towel for 1–2 minutes.
    38. For vegetarian version, substitute chicken with mixed vegetables or paneer and follow same method.
    39. To make the biryani spicier, increase green chilies and red chili powder in the marinade.
    40. To reduce oil, use less ghee and more non-stick cooking spray while frying onions.

Fish Fry

ingredients

  1. 500 g fish fillets or whole fish (cleaned and patted dry) — e.g., tilapia, pomfret, or mackerel
  2. 1 tsp turmeric powder
  3. 1 tsp salt (adjust to taste)
  4. 1½ tsp red chili powder or paprika (milder option)
  5. 1 tsp ground black pepper
  6. 1 tsp garlic powder or 2 cloves garlic, minced
  7. 1 tsp ginger paste (optional)
  8. 2 tbsp lemon or lime juice
  9. 3 tbsp rice flour, cornflour, or all-purpose flour (for a light crust)
  10. 2–3 tbsp oil for frying (vegetable/peanut/sunflower)
  11. Fresh cilantro or lemon wedges for garnish (optional)

Start to make the food

  1. Rinse the fish and pat dry with paper towels.
  2. Make shallow slits on both sides of whole fish (if using fillets, score lightly).
  3. In a bowl, mix turmeric, salt, red chili powder, black pepper, garlic, ginger (if using), and lemon juice to form a marinade.
  4. Rub the marinade all over the fish, getting it into the slits/edges. Let it rest for 15–30 minutes. (If short on time, you can fry immediately.)
  5. Just before frying, lightly dust the fish with the flour of your choice—this helps form a crisp outer layer.
  6. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium-high heat. Test with a small piece of batter or a small scrap of fish — it should sizzle.
  7. Place the fish gently into the pan. Fry for about 3–5 minutes per side (depending on thickness) until golden and cooked through. Avoid overcrowding the pan.
  8. When cooked, transfer to a rack or paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  9. Garnish with chopped cilantro and lemon wedges. Serve hot with rice, salad, or chutney.
